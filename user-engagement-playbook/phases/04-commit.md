# Phase 4 – Commit

Two events. The first verifies the design says what stakeholders meant. The second is the commitment gate: leadership signs, resources commit, and the build begins. This phase is short and unglamorous, and skipping it is the single most expensive shortcut in enterprise delivery.

---

## Configuration Design Review

**Purpose:** Confirm the solution as designed reflects stakeholder intent before any build begins.

The read-back. The team plays back the design in the stakeholders' own language: "here is what you asked for, here is how the system will do it, confirm or correct." Misunderstandings caught here cost a conversation. The same misunderstanding caught in UAT costs a release.

**Participants:** System owners, key stakeholders, solution architect, BA/product lead.

**Cannot run without:** The elaborated backlog, design artifacts a non-technical stakeholder can actually read, and the original framing outputs to trace against.

**Must produce:**
- Documented stakeholder confirmation, or documented corrections with owners
- An updated design baseline
- A decision log entry for every "confirm or correct" that went to correct

---

## Roadmap Commitment Session — GATE

**Purpose:** Secure leadership sign-off on sequence, resourcing, and accountable ownership of each workstream.

This is a **gate event**: sprint 1 does not begin until it is complete. A program that starts building while leadership is "still reviewing the roadmap" has volunteered to re-plan mid-build, with the meter running.

**Participants:** Executive sponsors, program leadership, workstream owners. The people whose names go next to commitments.

**Cannot run without:** The confirmed design baseline, a sequenced roadmap with dependencies visible, and a resourcing picture honest enough to sign.

**Must produce:**
- Signed (literally, on the record) leadership endorsement of sequence and scope
- A named, accountable owner for every workstream
- Agreed governance rhythm for the build: who meets, how often, with what decision rights

**Facilitation note:** If an executive won't put their name next to a workstream, that workstream is not resourced, no matter what the slide says. Better to discover that at the gate than at sprint 4. That's what the gate is for.
