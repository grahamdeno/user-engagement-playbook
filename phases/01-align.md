# Phase 1 – Align

One event, held first, before anyone else's calendar gets touched. Discovery conducted before leadership agrees on the objective produces beautifully documented findings about the wrong problem.

---

## Stakeholder Alignment Session

**Purpose:** Align senior leaders on priorities, constraints, and what success actually looks like.

This is the leadership conference. You are not gathering requirements here; you are surfacing the disagreements that already exist among the leadership so they get resolved in a conference room now instead of derailing sprint 6 later. Every program has them: two directors with different definitions of done, an unstated budget ceiling, a system somebody is quietly protecting.

**Participants:** Executive sponsors, system/business owners, program leadership. Small by design. This is the one event where "the more the merrier" is exactly wrong.

**Cannot run without:**
- A draft problem statement to react to (people align faster against a strawman than a blank page)
- Pre-read on program scope and known constraints
- The actual decision-makers in the room, not their representatives

**Must produce:**
- Endorsed problem statement and success criteria
- Named priorities and named constraints, on the record
- A decision-rights map: who owns what call when the phases ahead force one
- A parking lot of surfaced disagreements with owners and due dates

**Facilitation note:** The most valuable moment in this session is the uncomfortable silence after you ask two executives to reconcile their answers. Let it sit. The program pays for that silence now or pays for it at UAT with interest.
